Process & Design Thinking

I approached The Little UX Lab like a small UX case study - identifying an area of curiosity, prototyping in code, testing motion concepts, and documenting insights.

Framing the Exploration

  • How can motion communicate feedback and system status?
  • What timing and easing patterns feel natural?
  • When does animation shift from delight to distraction?

My Workflow

  1. Sketched out ideas focusing on motion and feedback.
  2. Built low-fidelity browser prototypes using pure HTML/CSS.
  3. Added lightweight JS for responsive and dynamic motion.
  4. Refined timing, easing, and accessibility considerations.

Animation Types Explored

  • CSS Approaches
    • Transitions - hover & focus states for smooth affordance feedback
    • Keyframes - continuous pulses, micro loops, and dynamic motion cues
    • Transforms - scale, expand, and subtle rotations to emphasize discoverability
    • Opacity & Visibility - fade-ins for tooltips and reveals
    • Pseudo-classes - interactive responses (:hover, :focus, :active)
    • Flex & Position Animations - reflows and card expansions for spatial feedback
  • Mapped to Experiments
    • Hover Card - Transition (hover feedback)
    • Pulse Button - Keyframe (looping pulse)
    • Expanding Tile - Transform (scale & reveal)
    • Toggle Reveal - Opacity / Visibility transitions
    • Tooltip Icon - Position + opacity transition
    • Input Feedback - Conditional state change + color transition

Key Learnings

  • Motion communicates meaning when applied purposefully.
  • Building in code early bridges concept and usability.
  • Even micro-level design can evoke macro-level understanding.

Next Steps

  • Explore accessibility-focused animations - ensuring motion aids understanding without causing distraction.
  • Introduce user-controlled themes and motion preferences (e.g., reduced motion mode).
  • Refine transitions for mobile interactions and touch feedback.
  • Expand the gallery to include more UX concepts like loading states, confirmations, and success feedback.
  • Conduct quick usability tests to evaluate whether animations communicate their intended principles effectively.

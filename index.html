About the Project

The Little UX Lab is a self-initiated exploration of microinteractions - the subtle motions and feedback moments that make digital experiences feel alive. Through this project, I combined my interests in visual design and interaction design to understand how playful, minimal interactions can enhance usability and delight.

Built entirely with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, this lab served as a hands-on experiment in designing responsive feedback, motion timing, and visual texture - using the web as a prototyping medium rather than just a final product stage.

TypeSelf-initiated UX exploration
FocusMicrointeractions, Motion Design, Visual Feedback
ToolsHTML, CSS, JavaScript
TimelineFall 2025

Design Intent

The Little UX Lab started from a simple question: how do tiny, thoughtful motions influence how people perceive an interface? Each component was treated as a micro-experiment - by prototyping directly in code, I connected design intent with implementation and tested how visual feedback affects user flow.
